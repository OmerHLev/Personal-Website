Why QA?

It's quite obvious that I'm interested in code, coding, and tech in general, but one might wonder: Why QA specifically?

First off, I believe I'm more suited for it. One of the core traits you need for QA is a mind for finding the faults in systems.
For me, it is always natural to look for edge cases, and try to find the problems in systems, plans, code, and even ordinary day-to-day things.
Therefore, I believe I can be a valuable addition to any QA team.

Second, I enjoy the systems-oriented thinking that can often be found within QA.
To me, it is more interesting to write a testing framewrok (whether manual or automated) aimed at ensuring a lack of bugs (as much as that is possible)
than it is to implement any specific feature within the system.

Lastly, it seems to me from friends and family within the High-Tech industry that QA as a field has a lack of serious long-term canidates.
I've been told many times that for many, QA is an entry way job, for those who eventually want to become junior developers themselves.
This causes a lack of high quality QA personnel. Since QA is interesting in and of itself to me, I believe I can contribute a lot in the short and long terms.
